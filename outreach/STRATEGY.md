# Scoop — LinkedIn Outreach Strategy

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Who buys Scoop

**Primary buyer:** VP of Sales, Head of Sales, Director of Sales Ops
- They buy tools for the team
- They care about pipeline velocity and deal win rates
- $10/seat is invisible to their budget

**Secondary buyer:** Individual AEs (Account Executives) at B2B companies
- They forward it to their manager when it works
- "Can we get this for the whole team?" = conversion event

### Company profile

- B2B SaaS, enterprise software, cloud infrastructure
- 50-500 employees (big enough to have AEs, small enough that sales ops isn't bloated)
- AEs manage 10-30 named enterprise accounts
- Selling to mid-market or enterprise (not SMB)
- Verticals that work best: cloud/SaaS, cybersecurity, fintech, insurance, manufacturing

### Signals that someone needs Scoop

- They post about losing a deal they should have won
- They share articles about "account-based selling" or "account intelligence"
- They complain about Google Alerts being useless
- They talk about competitive deals
- Their company recently hired a bunch of AEs (scaling = needs tooling)
- They mention "renewal risk" or "churn" in their content

## Outreach channels (in priority order)

### 1. LinkedIn organic content (weeks 1-4)

Post 2-3x/week. Never pitch. Show the value by demonstrating it.

**Content formats that work:**

**"The Scoop" format (your flagship):**
Pick a well-known company (Snowflake, Datadog, HubSpot). Run the Scoop pipeline on them. Post the result as a mini-brief:

> 🐶🗞️ If you're selling to Databricks this week, here's what you should know:
>
> Their new CTO [name] just came from [company]. He's an [event-driven architecture] guy.
> They posted 12 job openings for "platform engineering" in the last 2 weeks.
> Their VP of IT spoke at [conference] about migrating off [legacy system].
>
> If you walked into a call knowing this, you'd close faster than the rep who didn't.
>
> This is what Scoop does, every Monday, for your accounts.

**"The miss" format:**
Tell a story about a deal someone lost because they didn't know something:

> A rep I know lost a $200K renewal because their champion left 3 weeks before the QBR.
> They found out during the call.
> Google Alerts didn't catch it. LinkedIn didn't surface it.
> A 30-second scan of the right signals would have saved the deal.

**"The comparison" format:**
Show what a Google Alert gives you vs. what Scoop gives you for the same company. Screenshot both. The difference sells itself.

### 2. LinkedIn DMs (weeks 2-4)

**DO NOT cold pitch.** The sequence:

1. **Engage first** — Like/comment on 3-5 of their posts over 1-2 weeks
2. **Connect** with a note: "Hey [Name], I keep seeing your takes on [topic]. I'm building something for AEs and your perspective would be useful. Happy to connect."
3. **After they accept** (wait 2-3 days): Share a real Scoop output for one of their customers: "Ran this on [their company's likely customer]. Thought it might be useful for your next call."
4. **If they engage**: "We're giving early access to sales leaders who want to try it. Free, no strings. Want in?"

**Never do:**
- "Hi [Name], I noticed you're in sales..."
- Pitch in the connection request
- Send a wall of text
- Follow up more than twice

### 3. Sales communities (weeks 3-6)

**Where to show up:**
- Revenue Collective / Pavilion Slack
- Sales Hacker community
- r/sales on Reddit
- Modern Sales Pros
- Bravado

**How:** Answer questions about account research. When someone asks "how do you prep for enterprise calls?", describe the process and mention Scoop naturally. Don't drop links unprompted.

## LinkedIn search queries for finding prospects

```
"VP of Sales" "B2B" "SaaS"
"Head of Sales" "enterprise" "account executive"
"Director Sales Operations" "B2B"
"Sales Enablement" "account intelligence"
"Account Executive" "enterprise" ("AE" OR "strategic accounts")
"Revenue Operations" "B2B SaaS"
```

Filter by:
- Company size: 51-500
- Industry: Computer Software, IT Services, Financial Services
- Location: US, UK, France, DACH
- Posted in last 30 days (active on LinkedIn)

## Messaging templates

### Connection request (max 300 chars)
```
Hey {first_name} — I'm building an account intelligence tool for AEs. Your perspective on {something_specific_from_their_profile} would be really valuable. Happy to connect.
```

### First message after connection (wait 2-3 days)
```
Hey {first_name}, thanks for connecting.

I ran a quick test — pulled the latest signals on {one_of_their_likely_accounts}:

{paste_one_signal_from_scoop}

This is what we send every Monday. Free for now, takes 2 min to set up. Worth trying?
```

### Follow-up if no response (wait 5 days)
```
No worries if the timing's off. Just wanted to share this — we found {interesting_signal} about {company}. Thought it might be useful before your next call.
```

### If they say yes
```
Here's the link: https://noptus.github.io/scoop

Just drop your email, what you sell, and your top 10 accounts. First digest lands Monday.

Let me know what you think after the first one — brutal honesty welcome.
```
